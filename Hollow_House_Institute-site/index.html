Behavioral Drift

Behavioral Drift is the gradual, compounding divergence between how an AI system behaved at deployment and how it behaves today. It is the core failure mode of agentic systems operating over time.

Punchline: Models don’t fail all at once — they fail a little every day.

Governance Debt

Governance Debt is the accumulated gap between how much oversight an AI system requires and how much oversight actually exists. Like technical debt, it compounds silently until it becomes a systemic risk.

Punchline: Every ungoverned decision becomes tomorrow’s liability.

Stop Authority

Stop Authority is the ability to halt, override, or constrain an AI system at the exact moment its behavior becomes unsafe, misaligned, or out of policy. Without it, drift becomes damage.

Punchline: If you can’t stop it, you’re not governing it.

Human-in-the-Loop (Governance Context)

Governance Context defines when, where, and how humans intervene in an AI system’s behavior. It is not about adding friction — it is about adding judgment.

Punchline: Humans don’t need to be in every loop — just the right ones.
